Roommate · Design System
v0.1 · April 2026

A calm, confident design language for holding houses accountable.

A clean, minimalist system borrowing the engagement psychology of Duolingo, the social restraint of BeReal, and the aesthetic polish of Linear. Every token lives here.

Principles

Six rules that shape every screen. Break one, and the product stops feeling like this product.

01

Clarity over cleverness

Every pixel earns its place. If we can't defend an element in one sentence, it goes.

02

Visible, not loud

Accountability is ambient. Streaks live next to you, not in front of you.

03

Gentle failure

No red, no shame, no broken streaks. Houses are relationships, not leaderboards.

04

Never rank, only share

We show percentages of a whole, never 1st / 2nd / 3rd. Comparison, not competition.

05

Motion ≤ 300ms

Animations confirm action; they don't perform it. Respect prefers-reduced-motion.

06

Dark-mode first

Design for darkness. Invert for light. The accent must pass WCAG AA on both.

Color

Built in OKLCH for perceptual uniformity. One accent, three semantic tokens, nine neutrals. No routine red.

Voice & microcopy

Language is part of the system. What we say — and don't say — shapes how the app feels.

We say

  • "Needs attention"
  • "Paused"
  • "Catch up when you're ready"
  • "Sam covered dishes tonight"
  • "Your house is on a 28-day streak"

We don't say

  • "Failed"
  • "You broke your streak"
  • "Overdue!!!"
  • "You're in last place"
  • "Buy streak freeze"
