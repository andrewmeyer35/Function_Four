# UX Notes

## Design Principles
1. **Fast to submit** — check-in should take under 3 minutes
2. **Warm not clinical** — feels like a group chat, not a productivity tool
3. **Accountability without shame** — missing a week is okay; don't punish it
4. **Mobile-first always** — most usage will be on phone

## Check-in Flow
1. User taps reminder notification or opens app
2. Sees this week's 4 prompts — one per F, swipeable
3. Types a few sentences (or quick-reacts)
4. Submits → lands in household feed immediately
5. Sees who else has checked in this week

## Feed Design Thinking
- Cards grouped by week (not flat reverse-chrono)
- Color-coded by F category (consistent across the app)
- Person's avatar + name prominent
- Reactions shown as floating emoji on the card
- "Still waiting on [name]..." indicator at top of current week

## Open UX Questions
- Submit all 4 Fs at once vs. one at a time throughout the week?
- Chronological feed vs. grouped by F category?
- How much visibility into other crews vs. just your household?
- Weekly prompts or biweekly to reduce fatigue?
- What does "checking in" look like on a home screen widget?

## Color Language (proposed)
- 💰 Financial → Amber / gold tones
- 💪 Fitness → Green tones
- 🎉 Fun/Friends → Blue tones  
- ❤️ Flirt/Fervier → Pink/rose tones
